# SOTP Method Notes

Use this pack for companies whose consolidated financials hide the fact that the business is
really several different valuation problems stitched together.

## Primary Method

Use SOTP when:

- different segments deserve different methods or peer sets
- the company has listed holdings, operating subsidiaries, or financial assets mixed with
  operating businesses
- corporate overhead, net cash, or holdco discount matter materially

## Secondary Methods

Use a consolidated DCF or market check when:

- the market still prices the company on a whole-company basis
- you need a sanity check against bottom-up value
- segment disclosures are incomplete and SOTP precision is overstated

## Focus Areas

- segment boundaries and what belongs in each unit
- ownership percentage and minority interests
- segment-specific valuation convention
- central cash, debt, pensions, and investments
- holdco discount justification and base

## Common Failure Modes

- double counting central items in both segment and holdco buckets
- valuing low-quality segment disclosures with false precision
- applying a holdco discount without defining the discountable base
- mixing enterprise-value and equity-value methods without a clean bridge
