# Regulated Assets Method Notes

Use this pack for businesses where the market convention is driven by regulated capital recovery
and allowed return, not by a generic operating-company `DCF`.

## Primary Method

Use `RAB` / rate-base valuation when:

- value is anchored to regulated asset base or rate base
- authorized capital structure and allowed return are visible
- market conventions reference `P/RAB`, `EV/RAB`, or regulated-equity-base value

## Secondary Method

Use `DDM` when:

- dividend policy is stable and visible
- payout is a meaningful part of the equity story
- terminal dividend growth can be tied to rate-base growth and regulatory recovery

## Focus Areas

- opening / closing rate base and growth
- allowed `ROE`
- equity ratio and leverage capacity
- regulatory lag and case timing
- capex recovery visibility
- dividend coverage and payout sustainability

## Common Failure Modes

- mixing enterprise-style debt subtraction into an equity-base valuation without isolating holdco
  leverage
- using `DDM` when payout is unstable or under-covered
- hiding non-regulated assets, holdco costs, or preferred claims inside a single plug
- relying on WACC logic without showing how allowed return compares with investor hurdle rate
