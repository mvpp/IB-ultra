# Perspectives And Bias

## Six Perspective Cross-Check

Use these as judgment overlays after the script outputs are complete.

### Quality Compounder

Ask:

- is the franchise stronger five years from now?
- are ROIC and reinvestment durable?
- does the moat widen with scale?

### Imaginative Growth

Ask:

- how large is the upside if the growth engine really compounds?
- which assumptions are non-consensus but plausible?
- what is the TAM or product-curve risk?

### Fundamental Long / Short

Ask:

- where is the market wrong over the next 1-3 years?
- what is the cleanest variant view?
- what data point will change the debate?

### Deep Value

Ask:

- what would a disciplined buyer pay for the whole business?
- which assets or cash flows are under-earning today?
- how much downside is protected by assets, cash, or normalized earnings?

### Catalyst-Driven

Ask:

- what event can close the valuation gap?
- what is the expected timing?
- what if the catalyst slips?

### Macro Tactical

Ask:

- how exposed is the thesis to rates, liquidity, FX, or regulation?
- is the timing dominated by macro rather than company execution?

## Bias Checklist

Before finalizing, check for:

- anchoring on a prior price target
- overconfidence in management narrative
- ignoring dilution or balance-sheet risk
- using peer multiples without peer-quality judgment
- treating cyclical peak earnings as steady state
- confusing a good company with a good stock at the current price

## Pre-Mortem Prompt

Assume the position underperforms badly over the next 12-24 months. Write down:

1. what went wrong operationally
2. what went wrong in the valuation
3. which signal we should have noticed earlier
4. whether the mistake was thesis, timing, or position sizing

## Red Flag Types

Flag explicitly when any of these appear:

- aggressive non-GAAP adjustments
- repeated misses masked by narrative
- dilution-dependent funding model
- weak governance or related-party concerns
- rising leverage with weakening cash conversion
- customer concentration without pricing power
