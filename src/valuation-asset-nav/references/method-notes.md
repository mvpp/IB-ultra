# Asset NAV Method Notes

Use this pack for businesses where the market convention is driven by reserve or resource value,
not by a generic operating-company `DCF`.

## Primary Method

Use asset `NAV` when:

- value is driven by asset-level reserves, production, or mine life
- commodity-deck assumptions are first-order drivers
- equity value is better understood as a bridge from asset value than from one consolidated
  terminal-state cash flow

## Secondary Methods

Use `P/NAV` market checks when:

- peer sets are reasonably comparable on reserve quality and commodity exposure
- market convention references `P/NAV`
- the user wants to compare underwritten `NAV` to market-implied confidence

Use commodity sensitivity when:

- valuation changes materially under plausible deck moves
- a single spot-price assumption would hide the real risk

## Focus Areas

- reserve / resource inventory
- production profile and decline
- price realization vs benchmark
- unit cash costs and sustaining capex
- tax / royalty and abandonment assumptions
- bridge from asset `NAV` to equity value

## Common Failure Modes

- treating undeveloped reserves as fully de-risked with no timing or capex burden
- mixing enterprise-value debt subtraction with an already after-financing equity `NAV`
- using peer `P/NAV` without checking jurisdiction, asset quality, or commodity mix
- hiding sustaining capex or abandonment costs in a generic corporate plug
